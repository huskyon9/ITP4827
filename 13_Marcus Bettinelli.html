He arrived, having been out of contract at Fulham, to add strength to our goalkeeping resources following the departure of Willy Caballero.

The 6ft 4in keeper spent over a decade at Craven Cottage, making 120 appearances for the Whites, and also represented the England Under-21 team earlier in his career.

The goalkeeper was regularly included among the Chelsea substitutes by Thomas Tuchel for cup competitions during the season, and in the Premier League during Edouard Mendy's absence at the Africa Cup of Nations during January.



It was during that month Bettinelli made his Blues debut from the start, helping us progress from the FA Cup third round with a 5-1 victory over Chesterfield at Stamford Bridge. He was also on the bench for both games of our first-ever FIFA Club World Cup triumph in February.

Bettinelli started his career at Fulham at the age of 14 and progressed through the ranks before making his club debut at the start of the 2014/15 season, following their relegation to the Championship.

Prior to that, he had spent time on loan in non-league with Dartford and in League Two at Accrington Stanley, gaining experience of senior football as a developing goalkeeper and winning the Young Player of the Year award at the Crown Ground.

Following the departure of David Stockdale in the summer of 2014, Bettinelli returned to the Cottage and established himself as Fulham’s first-choice keeper, making 45 appearances in all competitions.

Despite starting 2015/16 as the club’s number one, the Londoner picked up a knee ligament injury in the third game of the campaign and was sidelined for seven months. He returned in March and was back between the posts for the final nine games of the season, though Fulham’s promotion prospects continued to struggle as they finished the term 20th.

After establishing himself as first-choice goalkeeper at Fulham in 2014, Bettinelli earned international recognition with a maiden call-up to the England Under-21s squad.

It was four years later, during the first international break of the 2018/19 season, that the goalkeeper’s form in the Premier League was rewarded with a call-up to Gareth Southgate’s senior England squad, an achievement he repeated the following month in October, albeit without making an appearance for the Three Lions.
